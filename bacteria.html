Bacteria in the Microbiome

The gut microbiome of the average human contains trillions of microbes. Scientists estimate that about 99% of those microbes come from 30-40 species. The most common species in adults is Faecalibacterium prausnitzii. Studies have shown the this species prefers to feed on the fibers from chicory roots, wheat, onion, banana, garlic, and leek.
