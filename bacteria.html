Bacteria in the Microbiome

The gut microbiome of the average human contains trillions of microbes. Scientists estimate that about 99% of those microbes come from 30-40 species. The most common species in adults is Faecalibacterium prausnitzii. Studies have shown the this species prefers to feed on the fibers from chicory roots, wheat, onion, banana, garlic, and leek. Another common type of bacteria is Streptococcus thermophilus. These beneficial microbes are typically found in yogurt or other fermented dairy products. Lactobactillus is a common species with many subspecies. They can be found in a wide variety of fermented foods as well as in probiotic supplements.
